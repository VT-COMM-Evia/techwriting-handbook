Lectures

A lecture is the method through which instructors teach specific concepts of a given topic. When delivered through an online course, lectures are synonymous with tutorials.

While the medium through which you deliver a lecture will likely depend on the resources you have available to create the lecture, it is important to remember that the lecture should be dictated based on the needs of the concepts being taught. For example, some elements of punctuation and grammar may be easily taught and learned through a written document (delivered as a PDF). However, such concepts as rhetorical situation may be better delivered as a screencast or video. Regardless of the medium, a lecture should be as short as possible (for example, a video should be no longer than six minutes) and thus should cover a specific concept within a given topic.
Note: Your lessons do not have to include only lectures that you create. You can integrate written work and videos from other sources if you believe they are effective for the material being taught. However, it is important to ensure all material (including material from external sources) is accessible to students with disabilities.
