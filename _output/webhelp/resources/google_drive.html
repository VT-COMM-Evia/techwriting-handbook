Google Drive

Google Drive provides a place for students and faculty to save content in the cloud and then share that content (or collaborate on it) with others to whom they give permission.

Other Google apps (such as Google Docs and Google Slides) use Google Drive as the storage location for content developed with those apps (you can also save content to your local computer). Even if students do not want to use Google Drive to share or collaborate on content, it is a good (and free) tool for them to back up their work in the class. Since storage space is free for Virginia Tech students, you can reasonably require students to maintain backups of all their work so that students will not miss assignment deadlines because of data loss on their local computer.
